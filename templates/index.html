About the Project

This project provides a user-friendly platform for translating American Sign Language (ASL) hand signs into text using computer vision and machine learning.

It interprets signs captured either through a live camera feed or uploaded images by analyzing hand landmarks with models trained on ASL data. Users can also train new classification models using various algorithms and custom datasets, then compare their performance directly within the application. Our goal is to offer a practical tool for learning, communication practice, and accessibility enhancement.

Developed by Sajid Miya

Core Features

Real-time Recognition

Translate ASL signs directly from your webcam feed using a selected model and see the sequence build.

Image Upload

Get predictions for single or multiple uploaded images of ASL signs and view the combined result.

Train Models

Train custom classification models using various algorithms and your `.pkl` datasets. Models are saved for later use.

Compare Models

Evaluate and compare the performance (accuracy, speed) of different algorithms on a dataset without saving.

AI Powered

Leverages MediaPipe for robust hand tracking and scikit-learn models for efficient sign classification.
